Source Snippet

Market interventions often lead to unintended consequences. When a governing body mandates a price ceiling—a legal maximum on the price at which a good can be sold—it disrupts the natural equilibrium. If this ceiling is binding (set below the equilibrium price), the immediate effect is that the quantity demanded by consumers will significantly rise, while suppliers, disincentivized by lower potential revenue, will decrease the quantity they supply. This disparity invariably creates a shortage in the market, often leading to rationing mechanisms such as long lines or seller biases, rather than price determining allocation.
